Know Your Options

Cure Default

This means simply paying off the amount you are behind plus late fees and attorney fees.

Loan Modification

A loan mod can lower your payment and get you out of foreclosure by dealing with the default amount.

Keep Your Home California

3 programs are offered to either cure the default, lower principle balance, or pay the mortgage for 18 months.

Bankruptcy

A Chapter 13 is a repayment of debt on a monthly basis. If filed this can allow you to keep your home.

Sell Property

If you have equity you make money. If not, it's a short sale. Either way, you are out of foreclosure.

Other

Options include reverse mortgages, hard money loans, and litigation. Special circumstances apply.

Preforeclosure

Missing 3 or more mortgage payments. Late payments are generally reported to the credit bureaus and negatively affects the borrower's credit rating.

Notice of Default

This is a publically recorded document filed after a minimum of 3 mortgage payments have been missed. This officially marks the beginning of foreclosure. A law firm is now working on behalf of the lien holder (usually the mortgage company) to handle foreclosure proceedings. The document has a default amount listed. If this amount is paid (attorney fees may also be added) the default is "cured" and the account becomes current and is no longer in foreclose. The lien holder generally files a Release of Lis Pendons to signify the new status.

Notice of Trustee Sale

This publically recorded document is recorded a minimum of 90 days the Notice of Default was recorded. There is now an public auction scheduled to sell the property. The auction can be 21 days from the recording of the Notice of Trustee Sale. The borrower has up until 5 days prior to the auction to cure the default.
